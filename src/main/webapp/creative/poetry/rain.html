tao te ching rain

I would shatter your image to ice crystals
cold and beautiful
the fruit of dormant trees
waiting out seasonal disaster.

Your voice would be the song of the clouds
flashing and violent
the words of patient moisture
waiting out long periods of silence.

I would localize your destruction
to a brief moment of passionate
absence,
a diversionary touch of unfulfilled lust.

But your influence is pervading and persistant
as the waters from the sky
freeze and shatter boulders to sand
wash through mountains wearing rocks to pebbles.
