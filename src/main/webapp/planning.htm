Comments to Planning Commission, regarding draft resolution and minutes

We have a clarification request regarding the minutes of December 10 2003.

At that meeting, a 300 foot expansion of 85 Crescent was approved. After the meeting, an unexpected clause requiring earth movement insurance was added in a draft resolution was prepared by staff. However, there is no indication of this being part of the actual motion at the meeting, either in the official minutes, the voice recording, or a verbatim transcript of the voice recording we have made available to the planning department. If you wish a copy of that transcript, I have some with me.

Gordon has been gracious enough to join us to discuss reasons why earth movement insurance is not a requirement.

We would like to briefly describe why earth movement insurance is too onerous to apply to this 300 foot expansion.

1. The property at 85 Crescent will be embedded in chert, a stable substrate. This has been documented by our soils engineer, Vince Howes.

2. Earth movement liability insurance would cost us about $375,000 for 1 year of $3 million in coverage, $125,000 per million in coverage per year.  Since insurers do not perform soils analysis to determine price, their pricing assumes the worst conditions, and we would be paying for that--wasting a lot of money.

3. No projects of this size in Marin County have bought earth movement insurance and most insurance carriers will not supply it, according to our insurance broker, Hunter Insurance, and according to HRH Insurance in Novato. Our soils engineer indicates he is unaware of any earth movement insurance requirement on any projects except in situations of zero-setback. Gordon Sweeney has been present in conference calls confirming these issues.

4. Substantial earth movement monitoring, and a peer engineering review component are part of the (undisputed) conditions in the resolution.

Although we appreciate that Gordon Sweeney has some leeway on enforcing this unexpected condition, we believe we should also address the resolution issue formally to avoid problems if another city engineer or a different planner should take over at a later date.

We ask that the planning commission clarify its intent, and specifically request that it not require earth movement insurance as a requirement for this 300 foot expansion of 85 Crescent Ave.

Gordon is here to clarify his intent regarding this project.
