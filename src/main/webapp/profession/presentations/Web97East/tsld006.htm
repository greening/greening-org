Integration: What can you do?

    Passive: Understand customer needs

    • Tie web to direct marketing efforts
    • Understand marketing and branding issues
    • Use standard databases and internet protocols

    Active: Help customers win

    • Better targeting
    • Better inventory
    • Integrate with point-of-sale

Notes:

    None of us can really stop supply chain integration, so if you can’t beat ‘em, join ‘em. Let’s figure out what to do to help our pals integrate their supply chains faster than their competitors so we’ll all win the game.
    The passive approach says, “OK, my company isn’t ready to actively deploy something with our customers, but the minimum we should do is collect data.” Can you merge web information with your direct mail lists? You should integrate your web page with your other marketing efforts.
    Register your web page visitors and ask them for their customer number. Store the customer number in a cookie. Then track the pages they visit on your site and the purchases they make, so later you’ll have the information you need to personalize your site better.
    The active approach says, “Actively helping consumers will attract and keep them buying from us.” Now you exploit the information you’re gathering to help suggest products to customers visiting your site and plan new inventory efforts. Integrating with point-of-sale terminals allows you to provide personalized and targeted coupons that consumers will appreciate.
