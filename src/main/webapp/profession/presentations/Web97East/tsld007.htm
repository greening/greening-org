Notes:

    There’s another trend in the consumer world, and that’s massive data collection. Companies are saving a lot of data, then getting headaches thinking about how they can store it long-term.
    What kind of data are companies saving? Purchase data, of course. And they’re also getting and saving demographic information when you fill out warranty cards on new purchases. On the web, companies can save behavior and preference information.
    Unfortunately, a lot of companies are throwing data away too. And some important information never gets stored on a computer. For example, when a company sends out a catalog, very few save the SKU numbers of the products being offered. Some companies throw away information about which products were returned, as if no purchase was made.
    All of this information has future value. Saving it is a good idea, because it can help your company provide better service to your customers.
