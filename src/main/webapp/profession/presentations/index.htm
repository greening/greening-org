Many-to-One Marketing: High-Revenue Site Personalization
Dan Greening
Web97 East, Washington, DC, October 1, 1997

This talk begins with a discussion of market forces driving online commerce. All elements of the supply chain are beginning to deploy personalization and target marketing. I discuss personalization/targeting technology in general, with a focus on how LikeMinds technology fits into the personalization picture.  Then I discuss LikeMinds technology innovations in specific, and compare that technology to alternatives.

Direct Marketing on the Web
Dan Greening
Internet and Electronic Commerce 98, New York, April 27, 1998

This talk discusses the nature of direct marketing off and on the web. Targeting is important for direct mail, while personalization is essential for internet marketing. I begin by discussing direct marketing functions for mail campaigns and show how targeting and personalization play a role. I then discuss direct marketing for web applications, and show that targeting is relatively unimportant.  Three forms of personalization are commonly used: rule-based, Bayesian, and collaborative personalization.  I discuss collaborative personalization in detail, showing how LikeMinds overcame speed problems using a unique parallel processing mechanism.  I then summarize by discussing our experience with MovieCritic, our experimental test-bed for online marketing ideas.
