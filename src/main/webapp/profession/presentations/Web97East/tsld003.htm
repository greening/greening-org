Goal: Gain and retain customers

  • Anticipate consumer taste with minimal data

  • Improve wallet share with superior targeting

  • Distribute and use inventory effectively

Notes:

    So the goal of most direct marketers should be to get a better bang for their buck: improve the response rate by anticipating consumer taste and marketing to that taste.
    If you can customize the message to each consumer, we call that “personalization.” Personalization is something the web is uniquely positioned to do well. You can also do it in telesales, where you might up-sell—suggest to a caller buying a broach that they might also want to buy the cubic zirconium stick pin.
    Targeting is the flip side of personalization—it’s where we take an existing marketing message and send it only to those consumers most likely to respond.
    Finally, all marketers, both direct marketers and mass marketers, want to distribute their inventory as effectively as possible to get the greatest turnover.
    If you do a good job at personalization, targeting and distribution, you get a much higher response to your marketing, and therefore take home a lot more in profits.
