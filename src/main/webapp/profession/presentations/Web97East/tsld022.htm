  • Predicts most likely purchases

    • For groups

Notes:

    DirectSell is the offline version of WebSell. It targets products to individuals based on buying history, using similar algorithms to those found in WebSell.
    The differences between DirectSell and WebSell center around the types of data typically available. Where WebSell can get a lot of negative data, “you didn’t go to the blouse section,” in many cases, DirectSell gets no negative information, but just “you bought Jungle Red lipstick”. DirectSell typically deals with binary data: you either bought it or you didn’t.
    DirectSell mines existing sales history databases, then predicts which products are most likely to be purchased by an individual.
    DirectSell applies to direct mail and telesales operations, helping salespeople “up sell”, where you buy the broach and the salesperson suggests the stickpin, and in “cross selling”, where furniture purchases suggest kitchenware.
