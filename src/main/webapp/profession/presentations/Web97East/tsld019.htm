Notes:

    “Cold start” is a problem facing everyone that has new products to offer. How do you recommend the new book, movie, cosmetic, or car to a consumer if you have no previous customer experiences to draw from?
    You do it the same way a good salesperson does.
    When you walk into a bookstore and say “I want a book”, the salesperson says “well, what books do you like?” Suppose you say, “I liked Awakenings and Migraine.”
    Our salesperson thinks this: “Do I know anyone with similar taste? Yes, that person liked Passages.” and then says “People like you tend to like Passages.”
    Suppose our salesperson doesn’t know anyone like you. Then she can use the book database to find books with similar authors, subjects or publishers. And that’s what our archetypes technology does. It allows sites with product databases to leverage categorization data to help recommend books in cold start situations.
    The nice thing about this is you can always use it. I’m interested in books about Bayesian networks, but maybe two people have read the books I like. So a system like this will work for me using categories when buying obscure Bayesian network books, while helping me collaboratively when buying more general purpose books.
